Description

The HR boss wants to delve into the metrics of the two employee groups: those who left the company and those who still work for us. You decided to present the information in a table. Use groupby() and agg() to generate a DataFrame with metrics.

The HR boss asks for the following metrics:

  • the median number of projects the employees in a group worked upon, and how many employees worked on more than five projects;
  • the mean and median time spent in the company;
  • the share of employees who've had work accidents;
  • the mean and standard deviation of the last evaluation score

Remember that the guy from HR asks for those metrics for two different groups! Output the resulting DataFrame as a dictionary.

  9. Generate a table according to the boss's needs. Round all the numbers to two decimals. To round numbers to the second decimal place, you can apply the round(2) method to a DataFrame;
  10. Print the resulting table as a Python dictionary. To do so, use the to_dict() method.
