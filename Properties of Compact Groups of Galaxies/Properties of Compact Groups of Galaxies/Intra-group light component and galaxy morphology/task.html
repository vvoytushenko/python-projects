Description

The Sérsic index \(n\) and the numerical galaxy type \(T\) characterize galaxy morphology quantitatively. You can estimate the relation between the IGL component and the CG morphology by calculating the correlation of the IGL mean surface brightness with two means: \(n\) and \(T\).

Objectives

Calculate the mean Sérsic index and the mean numerical galaxy type for each group. To find a relation between the IGL and morphology of galaxies in groups, calculate the Pearson correlation coefficients between these values and the IGL mean surface brightness that is contained in the mean_mu column of groups.tsv. In other words, to complete this stage:

  • Continue working with galaxies_morphology.tsv from the previous stage. Group data by the Group column. The values of the new table will be the mean Sérsic index (n) and the mean numerical galaxy type (T). Create the columns mean_n and mean_T for obtained values with the help of the agg function from pandas.
  • Use the Group column and merge the data table with the one from groups.tsv that you preprocessed in Stage 1;
  • Plot scatterplots for the following value-pairs: mean_mu-mean_n and mean_mu-mean_T;
  • Conduct the Shapiro-Wilk Normality test for three variables: mean_mu, mean_n, and mean_T;
  • Calculate the Pearson correlation coefficients and the corresponding \(p\)-values for testing non-correlation. The null hypothesis of the test is that the considered populations are not correlated;
  • Print five floating-point numbers separated by one space: \(p\)-values for normality testing of mean_mu, mean_n and mean_T, and \(p\)-values for testing non-correlation for mean_mu-mean_n and mean_mu-mean_T.
