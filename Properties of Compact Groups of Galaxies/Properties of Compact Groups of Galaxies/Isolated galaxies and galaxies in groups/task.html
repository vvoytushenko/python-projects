Theory

Galaxy morphological classification is a system used by astronomers to divide galaxies into groups based on their visual appearance. To compare isolated galaxies and galaxies in groups, we will use the de Vaucouleurs galaxy classification system (column T) and the Sérsic index (column n).

Each class of galaxies in the de Vaucouleurs system corresponds to a numerical value. Values of the numerical Hubble stage \(T\) run from \(-6\) to \(10\). Negative numbers correspond to the early-type galaxies (ellipticals and lenticulars), and positive numbers indicate the late types (spirals and irregulars). You can learn more in the Hubble's Galaxy Classification Scheme video (YouTube) by Jason Kendall.

To explore the impact of high density in compact groups on the formation of early-type galaxies, consider the differences between the CGs of galaxies and a sample of isolated galaxies. In this sample, we have included galaxies that have no companions within a projected distance of \(1\) Mpc with a redshift difference lower than \(0.001\). To have better results, we only selected galaxies with the redshifts of \(0.01 ≤ z ≤ 0.06\).

The Sérsic profile is a mathematical function that describes how the intensity \(I\) of a galaxy varies with distance \(r\) from its center. The Sérsic surface-brightness profile takes the form:

\(I(r) = I_\mathrm{e} \exp \left(-\nu_\mathrm{n}\left[\left(\frac{r}{r_\mathrm{e}}\right)^{1/n} - 1 \right]\right)\,,\)

where \(I_\mathrm{e}\) is the surface brightness at the effective radius \(r_\mathrm{e}\), and \(n\) is the Sérsic index. \(\nu_\mathrm{n}\) is given by the solution of the equation

\(\Gamma(2\,n) = 2 \gamma(2n, \nu_\mathrm{n})\,,\)

where \(\Gamma(x)\) is the gamma function, and \(\gamma(s, x)\) is the incomplete gamma function.

Galaxies with a Sérsic index greater than \(2\) tend to be early-type or early-spirals galaxies. So, it may be a good idea to consider a fraction of such galaxies in both datasets.

Description

In this stage, you have two datasets with samples of isolated and grouped galaxies. The isolated galaxies' dataset contains a single column n with the Sérsic indexes. The dataset with compact groups of galaxies contains 4 columns: Group with group names, Name with galaxy names, n with the Sérsic indexes, and T with numerical Hubble stages:

Objectives

Load the datasets of isolated galaxies and galaxies in compact groups. Examine the histograms for the Sérsic index of isolated and galaxy groups in the picture below and try to plot the same chart. To discover the differences between the isolated galaxies and galaxies in CGs perform the Kolmogorov-Smirnov homogeneity test for galaxy distributions by the Sérsic index.

To complete this stage:

  • Read the datasets: galaxies_morphology.tsv and isolated_galaxies.tsv;
  • Plot the histograms for the Sérsic index for both datasets;
  • Calculate a fraction of galaxies with the Sérsic index \(n > 2\) for both datasets;
  • Perform the two-sample Kolmogorov-Smirnov test for equality for datasets and obtain a \(p\)-value. Note that you must use a two-sided alternative for this test. It means that the null hypothesis is that the two given samples are drawn from the same probability distribution;
  • Print three floating-point numbers separated by one space: fractions of galaxies with the Sersic index \(n > 2\) for groups' galaxies and isolated galaxies, and a \(p\)-value obtained from the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test.
